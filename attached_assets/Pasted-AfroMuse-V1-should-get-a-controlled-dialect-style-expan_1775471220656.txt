AfroMuse V1 should get a controlled dialect style expansion in the Lyrics Studio.

IMPORTANT:
Do NOT break the current V1 or V2 workflow.
Do NOT redesign the whole page.
Do NOT touch audio generation, project library, session engine, or unrelated systems.

Improve lyrical authenticity by expanding the language system beyond generic “Jamaican Patois” and “West African Pidgin” into more specific, musically useful writing style variants.

The current broad dialect modes are too generic.
That makes the lyrics sometimes feel inconsistent or AI-ish.

AfroMuse needs more specific writing identity modes so the model can generate more believable phrasing, emotional rhythm, and hook language.

1. ADD A NEW CONTROL IN LYRICS STUDIO
   Add a new field in the Lyrics Studio called either:

* Dialect Style
  OR
* Language Style

This should feel like a natural part of the current Lyrics Studio controls — not a new bulky form section.

* English
* Jamaican Patois
* West African Pidgin
  or current equivalents)

   Only show relevant style options depending on the selected language.

Show:

* Auto

* Jamaican Spiritual

B) If the selected language is West African Pidgin:
Show:

* Auto
* Naija Melodic Pidgin
* Ghana Urban Pidgin
