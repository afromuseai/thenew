## CRITICAL SONGWRITING RULES

### 1) Write songs, not explanations

The AI must stop over-explaining feelings or life situations.

Bad style:

* “I have been through many difficult experiences in life”
* “I know that God has been helping me through all my struggles”

Better style:

* “Pain don tire me”
* “Na God carry me”

Every line should feel like something someone could actually sing or record — not something written in an essay.

---

### 2) Hooks must be shorter and stronger

The generator should prioritize making choruses / hooks:

* shorter
* easier to remember
* more repeatable
* more chantable
* more emotionally immediate

The hook should usually contain the **keeper line** or a variation of it.

If the chorus is too long, too wordy, or too “written,” it should be simplified.

---

### 3) Stop generating fake motivational filler

The generator should avoid generic lines like:

* “I know one day I will make it”
* “I will continue to rise above”
* “No matter what happens I will never give up”
* “I am blessed and highly favored”

These lines feel fake and generic unless the song truly earns them.

Instead, use concrete, believable emotional or street-level phrasing.

---

### 4) Dialect must be built natively, not translated from English

This is extremely important.

If the user selects:

* Ghana Urban Pidgin
* Naija Pidgin
* Jamaican Patois
* street-pop / Afro-street style

…the AI must not write full English sentences and then sprinkle in slang.

Wrong:

* “I am feeling so much pain in my heart, abi?”

Better:

* “My chest dey hot”
* “Pain don choke me”
* “Mi heart heavy tonight”

The entire line construction should feel native to the dialect world.

---

### 5) Keep lines shorter and more musical

Most lines should be easy to phrase over a beat.

Avoid overly long lines with too many clauses.

The AI should favor:

* punchy short lines
* strong line endings
* natural pauses
* breath-friendly writing
* phrases artists can actually perform

---

### 6) Verses should move forward, not repeat the same pain 8 times

Each verse should feel like it is progressing.

A verse should ideally move through:

* scene
* feeling
* reaction
* consequence
* realization

Avoid saying the same emotional point repeatedly with slightly different wording.

---

### 7) Prioritize quotable lines

The AI should aim to include lines that feel:

* caption-worthy
* memorable
* screamable
* emotionally sharp
* artist-like

Examples of stronger line energy:

* “Dem laugh first, now dem dey watch”
* “Body weak but the hunger no die”
* “Silence loud when pain too much”
* “God know wetin man no see”

Do NOT copy these exactly unless naturally relevant — they are directional examples.

---

### 8) Match genre energy better

The writing style should adapt better to genre.

Examples:

**Afrobeats (emotional / romantic / spiritual):**

* smoother
* melodic
* intimate
* hook-focused

**Street-pop / Afro-street:**

* harder
* more direct
* more quotable
* more crowd-aware

**Dancehall / Patois:**

* rhythm-driven
* chantable
* naturally Jamaican in phrasing
* not fake “Google patois”

**Sad / heartbreak:**

* simple
* vulnerable
* believable
* not overly poetic

---

### 9) Bridge sections should matter

The bridge should not feel like random filler.

It should either:

* reveal something more vulnerable
* shift emotional perspective
* strip the song down before the final chorus

---

### 10) Outro should feel intentional

Do not end songs lazily.

The outro should feel like:

* a final emotional stamp
* a memorable repeat
* a closing thought worth leaving in the listener’s head

---
